Bob Ross Map

Ryan Mroczenski

This explainer describes a web map based on paintings, by the artist Bob Ross.

This map makes use of the prominent colors used in the paintings Bob Ross is known for, including white/gray, green, yellow, blue, and pink.

First, let's take a look at Taiwan, which gives us a sense of what the color scheme looks like on the map.

Milan, Italy, and many other locations throughout the map, show the close mix of yellow and green together, representative of the different trees along the shorelines in many of Ross's paintings.

Northern Minnesota portrays the use of colors most equally to the paintings, with gray, blue, and green being the predominant colors and slight touches of pink and yellow.

Finally, a look at Muncie, Indiana, home to the studio where the Bob Ross television series 'The Joy of Painting' was filmed, and where many of his paintings were created. Note the typeface of the labels on the map, as it is the same typeface used for both the credits and paint color inserts throughout the series.
